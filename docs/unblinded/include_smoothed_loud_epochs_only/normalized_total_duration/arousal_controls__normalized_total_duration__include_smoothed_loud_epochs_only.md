# Nonsocial Arousal-Control Panel

This compact control analysis asks whether DCZ shows a coherent downward shift across straightforward nonsocial activity and vigilance-related labels.

- Data selection: `Include smoothed loud epochs only`
- Aggregation mode: `normalized_total_duration`

## Definitions

- The panel uses simple nonsocial labels rather than composite exchange metrics.
- Values are expressed as percent of selected time so the same panel can be reused for the full session, quiet-mask branch, and loud-only branch.
- A broad arousal-suppression explanation would predict a fairly consistent downward shift across these control measures.

## Condition comparisons

- Forage/Search (% selected time): vehicle mean `7.992`, DCZ mean `11.129`, mean difference `3.137`, 95% CI `[-8.993, 17.915]`, exact permutation `p = 0.7358`.
- Travel (% selected time): vehicle mean `3.671`, DCZ mean `1.833`, mean difference `-1.839`, 95% CI `[-3.469, -0.135]`, exact permutation `p = 0.0648`.
- Rest/Stationary (% selected time): vehicle mean `6.396`, DCZ mean `7.959`, mean difference `1.562`, 95% CI `[-4.777, 8.205]`, exact permutation `p = 0.6942`.
- Vigilant/Scan (% selected time): vehicle mean `14.276`, DCZ mean `8.567`, mean difference `-5.709`, 95% CI `[-15.696, 2.899]`, exact permutation `p = 0.3159`.
- Attention to outside agents (% selected time): vehicle mean `12.992`, DCZ mean `24.127`, mean difference `11.135`, 95% CI `[-0.348, 27.196]`, exact permutation `p = 0.1156`.
- Scratch (% selected time): vehicle mean `1.021`, DCZ mean `0.738`, mean difference `-0.283`, 95% CI `[-1.482, 0.746]`, exact permutation `p = 0.6123`.

## Interpretation

- None of these nonsocial control metrics shows a clear DCZ-linked suppression across the analyzed sessions.
- The pattern is mixed rather than uniformly downward, which argues against a simple global hypo-arousal account for the social exchange effect.
