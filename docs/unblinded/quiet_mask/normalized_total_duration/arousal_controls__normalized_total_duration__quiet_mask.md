# Nonsocial Arousal-Control Panel

This compact control analysis asks whether DCZ shows a coherent downward shift across straightforward nonsocial activity and vigilance-related labels.

- Data selection: `Exclude smoothed loud epochs`
- Aggregation mode: `normalized_total_duration`

## Definitions

- The panel uses simple nonsocial labels rather than composite exchange metrics.
- Values are expressed as percent of selected time so the same panel can be reused for the full session, quiet-mask branch, and loud-only branch.
- A broad arousal-suppression explanation would predict a fairly consistent downward shift across these control measures.

## Condition comparisons

- Forage/Search (% selected time): vehicle mean `4.532`, DCZ mean `4.495`, mean difference `-0.037`, 95% CI `[-3.673, 3.391]`, exact permutation `p = 0.9852`.
- Travel (% selected time): vehicle mean `1.176`, DCZ mean `0.751`, mean difference `-0.425`, 95% CI `[-1.471, 0.304]`, exact permutation `p = 0.5894`.
- Rest/Stationary (% selected time): vehicle mean `5.010`, DCZ mean `10.698`, mean difference `5.688`, 95% CI `[0.030, 11.683]`, exact permutation `p = 0.1004`.
- Vigilant/Scan (% selected time): vehicle mean `2.977`, DCZ mean `4.425`, mean difference `1.447`, 95% CI `[-0.550, 3.412]`, exact permutation `p = 0.2005`.
- Attention to outside agents (% selected time): vehicle mean `0.508`, DCZ mean `2.433`, mean difference `1.924`, 95% CI `[-0.414, 6.142]`, exact permutation `p = 0.6903`.
- Scratch (% selected time): vehicle mean `0.354`, DCZ mean `0.684`, mean difference `0.330`, 95% CI `[-0.005, 0.687]`, exact permutation `p = 0.0979`.

## Interpretation

- None of these nonsocial control metrics shows a clear DCZ-linked suppression across the analyzed sessions.
- The pattern is mixed rather than uniformly downward, which argues against a simple global hypo-arousal account for the social exchange effect.
