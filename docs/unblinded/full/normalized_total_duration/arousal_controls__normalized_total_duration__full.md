# Nonsocial Arousal-Control Panel

This compact control analysis asks whether DCZ shows a coherent downward shift across straightforward nonsocial activity and vigilance-related labels.

- Data selection: `Full analyzed session`
- Aggregation mode: `normalized_total_duration`

## Definitions

- The panel uses simple nonsocial labels rather than composite exchange metrics.
- Values are expressed as percent of selected time so the same panel can be reused for the full session, quiet-mask branch, and loud-only branch.
- A broad arousal-suppression explanation would predict a fairly consistent downward shift across these control measures.

## Condition comparisons

- Forage/Search (% selected time): vehicle mean `4.652`, DCZ mean `4.843`, mean difference `0.192`, 95% CI `[-3.541, 3.540]`, exact permutation `p = 0.9279`.
- Travel (% selected time): vehicle mean `1.388`, DCZ mean `0.843`, mean difference `-0.545`, 95% CI `[-1.547, 0.158]`, exact permutation `p = 0.3083`.
- Rest/Stationary (% selected time): vehicle mean `5.128`, DCZ mean `10.467`, mean difference `5.340`, 95% CI `[-0.274, 11.311]`, exact permutation `p = 0.1235`.
- Vigilant/Scan (% selected time): vehicle mean `3.947`, DCZ mean `4.778`, mean difference `0.831`, 95% CI `[-1.386, 2.953]`, exact permutation `p = 0.4867`.
- Attention to outside agents (% selected time): vehicle mean `1.569`, DCZ mean `4.274`, mean difference `2.706`, 95% CI `[-0.348, 7.927]`, exact permutation `p = 0.3092`.
- Scratch (% selected time): vehicle mean `0.411`, DCZ mean `0.688`, mean difference `0.277`, 95% CI `[-0.071, 0.635]`, exact permutation `p = 0.1765`.

## Interpretation

- None of these nonsocial control metrics shows a clear DCZ-linked suppression across the analyzed sessions.
- The pattern is mixed rather than uniformly downward, which argues against a simple global hypo-arousal account for the social exchange effect.
