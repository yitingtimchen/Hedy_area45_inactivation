# Nonsocial Arousal-Control Panel

This compact control analysis asks whether DCZ shows a coherent downward shift across straightforward nonsocial activity and vigilance-related labels.

- Data selection: `Exclude vet-entry session`
- Aggregation mode: `normalized_total_duration`

## Definitions

- The panel uses simple nonsocial labels rather than composite exchange metrics.
- Values are expressed as percent of selected time so the same panel can be reused for the full session, quiet-mask branch, and loud-only branch.
- A broad arousal-suppression explanation would predict a fairly consistent downward shift across these control measures.

## Condition comparisons

- Forage/Search (% selected time): vehicle mean `4.652`, DCZ mean `5.358`, mean difference `0.706`, 95% CI `[-3.143, 4.135]`, exact permutation `p = 0.7406`.
- Travel (% selected time): vehicle mean `1.388`, DCZ mean `0.816`, mean difference `-0.572`, 95% CI `[-1.579, 0.128]`, exact permutation `p = 0.3329`.
- Rest/Stationary (% selected time): vehicle mean `5.128`, DCZ mean `10.566`, mean difference `5.439`, 95% CI `[-0.769, 12.137]`, exact permutation `p = 0.1465`.
- Vigilant/Scan (% selected time): vehicle mean `3.947`, DCZ mean `4.765`, mean difference `0.818`, 95% CI `[-1.495, 3.149]`, exact permutation `p = 0.5239`.
- Attention to outside agents (% selected time): vehicle mean `1.569`, DCZ mean `1.786`, mean difference `0.217`, 95% CI `[-0.583, 0.943]`, exact permutation `p = 0.6214`.
- Scratch (% selected time): vehicle mean `0.411`, DCZ mean `0.727`, mean difference `0.316`, 95% CI `[-0.065, 0.700]`, exact permutation `p = 0.1433`.

## Interpretation

- None of these nonsocial control metrics shows a clear DCZ-linked suppression across the analyzed sessions.
- The pattern is mixed rather than uniformly downward, which argues against a simple global hypo-arousal account for the social exchange effect.
